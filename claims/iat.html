JWT IAT Claim Explained

What is the JWT iat claim? Learn Issued At meaning, examples, and validation rules for iat in JSON Web Tokens.

Quick Answer

To JWT IAT Claim Explained, paste your token into our JWT Decoder, inspect the header and payload claims, then verify the signature with the JWT Validator. All processing runs locally in your browser.

What Is the iat Claim?

The iat (Issued At) claim is a Unix timestamp of when the token was created.

Example

{ "iat": 1700000000 }

How to Inspect

Paste any JWT into JWT Decoder to see the iat claim in the decoded payload. Use JWT Debugger for validation warnings related to this claim.

Validation Rules

Reject tokens issued too far in the future (clock skew).
